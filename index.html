Daniel Zika

Videographer & Designer


Ever since I got within reach of a camera, I have spent much of my free time taking pictures, shooting video, editing, and acting, always looking for new and striking visual ways to tell stories. My early education in visual storytelling came from a wide array of places, starting with my dad's hobby as a photographer, and moving on to my work as a child actor in multiple Kennedy Center Operas. In middle and high school, I continued acting in plays and skits, receiving competition honors from the Folger Shakespeare Library in 2015. In high school, I entered the realm of filmmaking, taking cinematography and editing classes, attending local film clubs with guest lectures and classes, and lots of time spent creating my own projects with friends. Since then, I have declared a Broadcast and Documentary Production Major within the Philip Merrill College of Journalism at the University of Maryland (the closest thing to a Film major at the institution), and coupled that with a Minor in Business, a combination I can already tell is priming me for a wide array of positions within the entertainment industry. On-campus, I work with the Big Ten Network producing live sports games and also with Maryland Athletics as a videographer and editor. I am also a part of the Maryland Filmmakers Club working on student-written, filmed, and produced projects. In my free time, I also create and edit video and social media content for my clothing brand called Good Looks Company and work on local projects both as a crew member, role/background actor, and model. No matter what role I take or what project or work is next for me, my aim is to provoke thought and engage the viewer using the visual medium that I feel has taught me the most in life.
